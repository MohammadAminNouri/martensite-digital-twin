# Article-derived data gap plan

The twin should not fill missing sample evidence with invented certainty. Literature and open datasets can fill model structure, default ranges, benchmark workflows and algorithm validation, but same-sample predictions require same-sample data.

## What articles/open sources can realistically fill

- Orientation relationships and variant/correspondence logic.
- Habit-plane candidate logic and interface interpretations.
- Benchmark parent-reconstruction algorithms.
- Open steel EBSD/CTF/dilatometry datasets for reconstruction validation.
- XRD/DSC methodology and reported ranges for NiTi and steel.
- Open-source analysis routes for EBSD, XRD, EDS, TEM/STEM, CALPHAD, mechanics and phase-field.

## What must come from our own data later

- Exact alloy chemistry and impurity content.
- Same-sample EBSD/TKD maps.
- Same-sample DSC/dilatometry curves.
- Same-sample XRD/synchrotron patterns and Rietveld results.
- Same-sample SEM/EDS/TEM evidence.
- LPBF thermal history, powder chemistry and porosity/residual stress.
- Mechanical curves and cyclic response.
- Independent validation samples.

## Why XRD, EDS, SEM and TEM matter

XRD gives phases, lattice parameters, retained austenite/B2/B19′/R fractions and peak shifts.
EDS/WDS gives chemistry, Ni/Ti ratio, alloying additions, impurities and segregation.
SEM/optical microscopy gives morphology, porosity, cracks, lath/plate context and melt-pool tracks.
TEM/STEM/SAED/4D-STEM gives nanoscale twins, precipitates, defects, local orientation and strain.

Without these, the app can perform crystallographic interpretation, but it cannot be defended as a full predictive digital twin.
